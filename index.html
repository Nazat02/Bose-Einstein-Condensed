The physics

From hot gas to quantum condensate

As temperature drops, the de Broglie wavelength of each atom grows until it overlaps with its neighbors — and the cloud stops behaving like a gas of particles and starts behaving like one giant matter wave.

~µK

Thermal gas

Atoms move randomly at high velocity, occupying a broad spread of momentum states — a classical Maxwell–Boltzmann cloud.

Cooling

Evaporative cooling

The hottest atoms are removed from the trap, lowering the average energy of those that remain — the cloud contracts and slows.

T → Tc

Condensation onset

Below the critical temperature, a macroscopic fraction of atoms suddenly collapses into the single lowest-energy quantum state.

BEC

Bose–Einstein condensate

A dense, coherent core emerges — a superfluid that can host quantized vortices and behaves as one giant matter wave.

Inside the lab

A playground for quantum phenomena

Every control maps to real physics — drive the system and watch the consequences ripple through the 3D cloud, the momentum distribution, and the phase-space readout.

Live temperature control

Drag the temperature slider and watch the condensate fraction shift in real time, with the momentum distribution reshaping from broad thermal wings to a sharp central peak.

Quantized vortices

Stir the superfluid core to nucleate quantized vortex lines — visible as swirling voids in the condensate density, a hallmark of macroscopic quantum coherence.

Collisions & collapse

Trigger atom-cloud collisions or a Bosenova-style collapse and watch the condensate fragment, evaporate, and re-form — with live event logging of every transition.

Momentum distribution

A live histogram tracks the momentum-space population — watch the bimodal distribution emerge as the condensate peak grows above the thermal background.

Density-surface view

Switch to an alternate 3D surface visualization mapping the cloud's density profile as a glowing thermal landscape — rotate and zoom freely.

Presets & time-of-flight

Jump straight to a formed BEC, a vortex lattice, a collapse scenario, or run a time-of-flight expansion to reveal the cloud's momentum-space signature.
